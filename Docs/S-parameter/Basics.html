S-/Z-/Y-/T-parameter conversion

1S to Z, Z to S

An N-port network is assumed. Its terminal voltages and terminal sink currents are defined as ii and ii. The impedance matrix Z is given and relates vi and ii as [v0v1vN1]=Z[i0i1iN1].

Considering S-parameter, the voltages are normalized by the square-roots of port impedances as v0=zP0u0,v1=zP1u1,vN1=zP(N1)uN1. Representing in matrix-vector notation, V=[zP0000zP10000zP(N1)]U=SZPU. and the currents are normalized by the square roots of port admittances as i0=yP0h0,i1=yP1h1,iN1=yP(N1)hN1. Representing in matrix-vector notation, I=[yP0000yP10000yP(N1)]H=SYPH. Including (3) and (5) into (1), it is modified as SZPU=ZSYPH

U=SZP1ZSYPH=SYPZSYPH

Decomposing the normalized voltages and the normalized currents into their incident and reflected components, U=UI+UR=(I+S)UI,H=HIHR=UIUR=(IS)UI.

Including (8),(9) into (7), (I+S)UI=SYPZSYP(IS)UI(I+S)(IS)1=SYPZSYP

Z=SZP(I+S)(IS)1SZP

(I+S)=SYPZSYP(IS)(I+SYPZSYP)S=(SYPZSYPI)S=(SYPZSYP+I)1(SYPZSYPI)

3S to T, T to S

Scattering matrix is applied to the 2N-port network, [BUAL]=[SULSURSLLSLR][BLAU]
